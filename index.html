Common Reasons for Unexpected INR Changes
🌿
Vitamin K spike or drop
Eating significantly more or less green vegetables than usual — even a single large salad day or skipping greens entirely can shift INR by 0.5–1.0 points within 2–3 days.
💊
Missed or extra warfarin dose
A single missed dose raises INR within 24–48h. Taking a double dose to compensate makes it worse. Always take the same time each day.
🍺
Alcohol
Even 1–2 drinks can raise INR by inhibiting warfarin metabolism. Heavy drinking can cause dangerous spikes. Even moderate alcohol is unpredictable on warfarin.
🤒
Illness, fever or diarrhoea
Fever increases warfarin's effect (raises INR). Diarrhoea reduces vitamin K absorption. Both can cause significant unexpected INR changes.
💊
Other medications (including OTC)
Antibiotics, antifungals, aspirin, ibuprofen, omeprazole, many supplements (fish oil, vitamin E, turmeric, ginger) all interact with warfarin. Always tell your doctor about any new medication.
🧄
Herbs and Indian spices (in large amounts)
Ginger, garlic, turmeric, fenugreek (methi), coriander and cumin can enhance warfarin's effect in medicinal quantities. Cooking amounts are generally safe if consistent.
☀️
Seasonal / dietary pattern changes
Festival seasons, travel, eating out more, or change in appetite due to weather often change diet patterns without conscious awareness — leading to unexpected INR shifts.
